About

dsanotes.com

We're here to make DSA make sense.

Most DSA resources assume you already know things. They jump straight into "obviously, the time complexity is O(log n)" without explaining why — and leave you staring at the screen wondering if you missed a step.

You didn't miss anything. The resource just didn't explain it well enough.

DSANotes was built to fix that. Every article starts from zero assumptions, explains the why before the how, and ends with an "oh, I get it now" moment. No hand-waving, no skipped steps, no jargon without explanation.

Who it's for

DSANotes is for:

  • College students preparing for coding interviews
  • Self-learners who find other DSA resources too dense or too fast
  • Anyone who's opened a DSA textbook, felt completely lost, and closed it again
  • Developers who learned to code but never properly studied DSA

If you know basic programming — variables, loops, functions — you have everything you need to start here.
